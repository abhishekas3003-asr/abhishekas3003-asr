### Selected work

**Financial & growth analysis — Suprajit Engineering (FY21–FY25)**
Revenue nearly doubled while profit fell 30% — but the profit drop was a one-off
insolvency charge, not the business breaking. Excluding it, underlying EBITDA
grew 23%. Five years of annual reports, validated in SQL, presented in Power BI.
→ [repo](LINK_SUPRAJIT)

**Customer retention economics — H&M (4.7M transactions)**
45% headline churn looked alarming — but the customers leaving were the ones who
barely spent. The top 10% of customers drove 51% of revenue; one-time buyers, a
third of the base, drove under 5%. Reframed retention from "how many are leaving"
to "how much revenue is actually at risk," and isolated the 10.8K-customer
segment that mattered.
→ [repo](LINK_HM)

**Marketplace retention diagnosis — Olist, Brazil**
A marketplace where 97% of customers never came back. Tested the obvious culprit
— late deliveries — and killed it: fixing every late delivery would have
recovered under 0.05% of lost customers. The retention problem was structural,
built into the model, not an operations failure.
→ [repo](LINK_OLIST)
